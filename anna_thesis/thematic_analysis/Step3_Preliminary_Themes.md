This document presents the preliminary themes developed from the comprehensive coding of 19 interview transcripts. Codes have been grouped into meaning clusters based on shared conceptual content relating to trust, effectiveness, and the trust-effectiveness link in AI vs. human career coaching.

## Theme Clustering Process

### Cluster 1: The Architecture of Trust

**Codes grouped:**
- trust-drivers-list/comprehensive
- confidentiality-important
- feeling-seen
- competence-builds-trust
- challenge-without-shame
- questions-before-advice
- non-judgment-valued

**What unites it:**
Participants consistently describe trust as multi-dimensional, requiring competence, emotional safety, and contextual understanding. Trust is not a single variable but an architecture built from specific conditions.

### Cluster 2: The Mechanics of Distrust

**Codes grouped:**
- distrust-drivers-list
- salesy-approach-distrusted
- template-advice-distrusted
- dismissal-breaks-trust
- overconfidence-distrusted
- personality-judgment

**What unites it:**
Distrust is triggered by specific behaviors: dismissiveness, generic/template approaches, commercial pressure, and personality labeling. These violations are often immediate and hard to repair.

### Cluster 3: AI as Safe but Shallow

**Codes grouped:**
- AI-non-judgment
- AI-for-writing-and-prep
- AI-reduces-blank-page
- AI-lacks-context
- AI-overconfidence-concern
- tool-not-authority

**What unites it:**
AI provides psychological safety through non-judgment but lacks contextual depth. Participants trust AI for tasks but not for truth or emotional holding.

### Cluster 4: Humans for Depth and Stakes

**Codes grouped:**
- human-for-context/reality-check
- human-for-emotional-support
- accountability-requires-human
- human-sees-patterns
- human-for-high-stakes

**What unites it:**
Humans are valued for emotional presence, accountability, contextual knowledge (especially local/industry-specific), and high-stakes decision support.

### Cluster 5: The Trust-Effectiveness Mechanism

**Codes grouped:**
- trust-enables-depth
- trust-as-gateway
- trust-as-multiplier
- distrust-blocks-action
- trusted-not-effective examples
- effective-not-trusted examples

**What unites it:**
Trust and effectiveness relate but are not identical. Trust enables depth of disclosure, which improves advice quality. However, trust without structure can be ineffective, and effectiveness can occur with partial trust for bounded tasks.

### Cluster 6: The Hybrid Ideal

**Codes grouped:**
- hybrid-preferred
- AI-draft-human-validate
- AI-for-preparation-human-for-emotional
- complementary-roles
- timing-of-modalities

**What unites it:**
Nearly all participants prefer a hybrid model where AI handles preparation, structure, and iteration while humans provide emotional support, accountability, and contextual validation.

### Candidate Theme 6: "The Hybrid Ideal"

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Candidate Theme Name** | The Hybrid Ideal: AI for Preparation, Humans for Pivotal Moments |
| **What Unites It** | 18/19 participants prefer a hybrid model where AI handles preparation, structure, and follow-up while humans handle emotional support, accountability, and high-stakes decisions. The sequence matters. |
| **Related Codes** | hybrid-preferred, AI-draft-human-validate, AI-for-preparation-human-for-emotional, complementary-roles, timing-of-modalities, ideal-hybrid-workflow |
| **Strong Extracts** | |

**Extract 1 (Laura #2, Line 5127):**
> "Week one, AI helps you map options, values, criteria. Then you meet a human coach... Then AI helps you build scenarios... Then a human helps you reality-check... Then AI helps you with execution."

**Extract 2 (Amir, Line 4934):**
> "AI for preparation, reflection, and follow-up. Human for pivotal moments... AI helps me clarify what I feel and what I want. Then human helps with contextual advice and accountability."

| Sub-theme 6b | AI as Post-Work: Integrating and tracking after human sessions |
